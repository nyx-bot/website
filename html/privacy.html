The developers ("us", "our", "the developers") of Nyx ("bot",
"software", "service") do not require the storage any specific data from
the user ("you", "the user", "member") by default, as the majority of
the configuration that lets the bot deliver its features have a default
value that allows for it to run as it was originally intended.

To be fully transparent, the only data that is stored on
Nyx's own services boils down to user avatars, and uploaded media, which
powers the software's logging functionality for server moderation
purposes; you can easily opt-out of providing this data to the service
by disabling certain log types (as of now; a new backend is in the works
to fully disable the feature itself while keeping logs enabled)

Any data that was provided to Nyx is never
sold to any third parties, or shared in general for that matter, and we
do not gain any benefits or revenue whatsoever by holding any data
provided by the user. Everything that is uploaded to Nyx's services is not stored beyond a week,
no matter the circumstance. In other words, we do not share your media
with anyone else ourselves -- the only way for media hosted on our
services to branch out to other people is through direct connections, or
people who have the direct access to the logs.

There are precautions put in place by us
within the software to limit access to the servers in which your data is
stored to only clients that require those servers in order to bring you
the software. However, in the extremely rare chance of a data breach,
you will be notified within 1-2 business days with everything we know
about the incident.

This privacy policy is subject to change at
any time, and by continuing to use the software, you agree to abide by
these terms. If there are any questions, concerns, or data deletion
requests to be made apparent to the Nyx team, please contact Syl (owner
and manager of the services) at syl@nyx.bot.
